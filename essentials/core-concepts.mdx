## Ownership Coins

Ownership coins are tokens that give holders verifiable, enforceable ownership with onchain governance. Unlike prior-era tokens that promised ownership but delivered speculation, ownership coins are structurally different: treasury is publicly tracked, governance is enforced onchain, and spending is linked to approved proposals.

01Resolved tracks all ownership coins launched through the MetaDAO ecosystem.

## Futarchy

Futarchy is a governance system where decisions are made by markets rather than token votes. Instead of voting yes or no on a proposal, token holders trade in conditional markets. The market outcome — based on the token's price — determines whether the proposal passes.

The underlying logic: if traders believe a proposal will increase the DAO's value, the pass market price will exceed the fail market price, and the proposal is approved.

## Decision Markets

A decision market is a pair of conditional markets opened for each governance proposal: a **pass market** and a **fail market**. Traders buy or sell the governance token in each. The markets run for a fixed period, and the TWAP of each market at the end determines the outcome.

## TWAP

The TWAP (Time-Weighted Average Price) is the average price of the governance token over the decision market window, calculated as a time-weighted mean. It is more resistant to manipulation than a spot price. The TWAP starts accumulating after an initial delay (typically 24 hours after the market opens).

## Pass Edge

Pass edge is the percentage difference between the pass TWAP and the fail TWAP at a given moment.

**Formula:** `(Pass TWAP - Fail TWAP) / Fail TWAP × 100`

A positive pass edge means the market believes the proposal will pass. The required pass edge is set by the DAO's `passedThresholdBps` parameter (e.g. 300 bps = 3%).

**Example:** If `passAndFailTWAPSpread` is `3.11%` and `twapThreshold` is `-3%`, the `twapThresholdMargin` is `6.11%`.

## Economic Direction in Decision Markets

Understanding how trades are classified economically is critical for interpreting tilt and conviction metrics.

<Warning>
  **Fail-sell is economically pro-Pass.** This is counterintuitive but important: selling the fail conditional token reduces liquidity in the fail market, pushing the fail price down — effectively betting on the proposal passing. All tilt and conviction metrics account for this.
</Warning>

| Direction | Economic Alignment |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Pass-Buy | Pro-Pass |
| Fail-Sell | Pro-Pass |
| Fail-Buy | Pro-Fail |
| Pass-Sell | Pro-Fail |

## NAV (Net Asset Value)

NAV is the value of the DAO's treasury divided by the circulating token supply. It represents the per-token book value of the underlying assets.

**Formula:** `Treasury Value / Circulating Supply`

A token trading at a premium to NAV means the market is pricing in the DAO's future earning potential beyond its current assets. A discount to NAV suggests undervaluation or uncertainty.

## mNAV

mNAV is the ratio of the token's market capitalization to the DAO's total treasury value — similar to price-to-book ratio in traditional finance.

**Formula:** `Market Cap / Treasury Value`

A high mNAV can signal relative overvaluation compared to treasury assets. A low mNAV can signal relative undervaluation.

<Note>
  **Treasury value** includes assets held directly in DAO wallets **and** the value of assets held in LP positions.
